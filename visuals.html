Treemaps

http://www.cs.umd.edu/hcil/treemap/

Treemap is a space-constrained visualization of hierarchical structures. It is very effective in showing attributes of leaf nodes using size and color coding. Treemap enables users to compare nodes and sub-trees even at varying depth in the tree, and help them spot patterns and exceptions. Treemap was first designed by a friend Ben Shneiderman during the 1990s.

MIC Data Mining without Prejudice

http://web.mit.edu/newsoffice/2011/large-data-sets-algorithm-1216.html

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Onbn285lris#t=211

My highschool friend Yakir and his brother developed a new technique for finding relationships between variable in large datasets that makes no prior assumptions about what those relationships might be. It analyzes large sets of data and considers every pair of variables in a dataset.
